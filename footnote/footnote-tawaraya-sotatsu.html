"Tawaraya Sotatsu"

He is an artist of the early Edo period (17th century), and one of main artists of Japanese painting history.
He has been respected as the cofounder of "Rimpa(one of plastic arts school of Japan)" with "Hon'ami Koetsu" who flourished in the same period.
"Re-creation and quote of classic works", "the wonder of construction and trimming", "Paint under-designs in gold and silver", "influence from the Chinese ink painting" are his characteristic style.
However, compared to the impact in history and the height of its popularity, there are many questions regarding his biography, and moreover, it is not even known dates of birth and death.
As his masterpieces, there are "Fujin-Raijin-Zu-Byobu(Wind God and Thunder God Screens)" and "Matsushima-Zu-Byobu(Waves at Matsushima)".
"Fujin-Raijin-Zu-Byobu(Wind God and Thunder God Screens)" has been said to be his symbolic work, and it is designated a national treasure of Japan.
"Matsushima-Zu-Byobu(Waves at Matsushima)" is owned by Smithsonian Freer Gallery of Art in Washington DC,United States of America, and it is getting a high reputation abroad.
