Cookies

Our web sites use "cookies." A cookie is a file on your computer or other devices to store a small amount of data sent to your browser from a web server. We use cookies to improve the quality of our service when you visit our web sites and other web sites of interest to you. We also use cookies to remind us of who you are, tailor our items, services, and advertising to suit the personal interests of you and others, estimate our audience size, assist our online merchants to track visits to and sales at our web sites and to process your order, track your status in our promotions, contests, and sweepstakes, and/or analyze your visiting patterns.

In addition, this Web Site uses cookies on your shopping cart and forms for contact information. By enabling cookies, you will be able to keep items you want in your shopping cart and to skip entering your contact information from your second order. We recommend that you turn cookies on when you shop on our web sites.

Security

Our web sites use an online shop platform and server provided by our Service Provider Future Shop Co., Ltd., which uses Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) to guard your information delivered to us electronically. SSL encrypts your name and address, so only YAMADA HEIANDO and Future Shop are able to decode your information.

As for your credit card number, it is encrypted and sent to a dedicated payment server run by another Service Provider Future Commerce Co., Ltd. It is not stored in our own systems in any way.

Unfortunately, however, no data transmission over the Internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. As a result, while we strive to protect your contact information, to the extent permitted by law, we do not guarantee or warrant the security of any information you transmit to or from our web sites, and you do so at your own risk.

We urge you to keep any password that you establish with us in a safe place and not to divulge it to anyone. Also remember to log off your account and close your browser window when you have finished your visit. This is to ensure that others cannot access your account, especially if you are sharing a computer with someone else or are using a computer in a public place, such as a library or an Internet cafe.
