California Roll

  • Ready in: 30 minutes
  • Yield: 1 sushi roll
  • Serves: 1 person

    INGREDIENTS

    • Sushi Rice
    • 1 cup rice
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 tablespoon seasoned rice vinegar
    • 1 sheet nori (seaweed paper)
    • Fillings
    • ½ avocado
    • ½ small cucumber
    • 1 ½ ounces crabmeat or imitation crabmeat
    • sesame seeds (black and/or white)

    RECIPE

    for
    SUSHI RICE

  1. Rinse rice under cold water and drain thoroughly. Water should be cloudy at first. Repeat until water becomes clear.
  2. Put the rice into a saucepan that has a lid. Add 1 cup of water. Bring to the boil and let it simmer for 15 minutes, until all water has been absorbed. Stir occassionally to prevent sticking.
  3. Remove the rice from heat. Cover with a lid and let it sit for 10 minutes.
  4. Spread the rice on a flat tray.
  5. Dress the rice with seasoned rice vinegar. Stir with a spoon to blend.
  6. Cool the rice by fanning. Fan with a magazine until the rice is room temperature.

    RECIPE

    for
    ROLLING

  1. Place a bamboo mat on the cutting board. Place on top of it one half sheet of nori (seaweed paper) with the rough side up.
  2. Dip your fingers into a bowl of water to moist them. This will prevent sushi rice from sticking onto.
  3. Grab a handful of sushi rice and spread gently over nori. Make sure to cover all edges. Leave the layer ½ cm thick.
  4. Sprinkle on sesame seeds.
  5. Flip the nori to show the smooth, bare side.
  6. You are now ready to place your fillings! Place them sideways so that they run the length of nori. It would make the rolling process easier if you have softer fillings (like avocado) closer to you.
  7. Put your thumbs under the bamboo mat close to you and other fingers on the opposite side of the fillings. Roll both the mat and nori towards your fingers.
  8. Grip the bamboo mat and press lightly with both hands.
  9. Repeat 7-8 until all fillings are tucked in. You'll have a complete sushi roll!
  10. Cut into 6-8 pieces with a wet blade. Serve with a little dish of soy sauce.

Sushi Ball

  • Ready in: 40 minutes
  • Yield: 6 sushi balls (3 varieties × 2 each)
  • Serves: 1 person

    INGREDIENTS

    • Sushi Rice
    • 1 cup rice
    • 1 cup water
    • 1 tablespoon seasoned rice vinegar
    • 1 sheet nori (seaweed paper)
    • Prosciutto Fillings
    • 2 slices prosciutto
    • ½ fig
    • water pepper
    • parsely
    • Avocado Fillings
    • 2 slices avocado
    • wasabi
    • pumpkin seeds
    • salt
    • black pepper
    • olive oil
    • Salmon Fillings
    • 2 slices smoked sallmon
    • 2 slices lemon
    • 2 shiso branches
    • parsely

    RECIPE

    for
    SUSHI RICE

  1. Rinse rice under cold water and drain thoroughly. Water should be cloudy at first. Repeat until water becomes clear.
  2. Put the rice into a saucepan that has a lid. Add 1 cup of water. Bring to the boil and let it simmer for 15 minutes, until all water has been absorbed. Stir occassionally to prevent sticking.
  3. Remove the rice from heat. Cover with a lid and let it sit for 10 minutes.
  4. Spread the rice on a flat tray.
  5. Dress the rice with seasoned rice vinegar. Stir with a spoon to blend.
  6. Cool the rice by fanning. Fan with a magazine until the rice is room temperature.

    PROSCIUTTO
    sushi ball

  1. Scoop rice with an ice cream scoop.
  2. Score a half fig in the middle. Place on the sushi ball.
  3. Cover the entire sushi ball with a prosciutto slice.
  4. Slice the prosciutto along the fig score. Open a little and stuff water pepper and parsely.

    AVOCADO
    sushi ball

  1. Scoop rice with an ice cream scoop.
  2. Put wasabi and avocado slice on the sushi ball. Wrap in plastic wrap and round with hands.
  3. Sprinkle on pumpkin seeds, salt, and pepper. Dress with olive oil.

    SALMON
    sushi ball

  1. Scoop rice with an ice cream scoop.
  2. Wrap a slice of smoked salmon vertically around the sushi ball. Sprinkle parsely on the sides.
  3. Put a lemon slice and a shiso branch on top.
  • "Seasoned" rice vinegar... What if I can't find one at stores?
  • You can easily make "seasoned" rice vinegar with just 3 ingredients. Combine 1 tbsp each of vinegar, sugar, and salt, and you're done!
  • I'm having difficulty shaping sushi balls... Any tips?
  • Ice cream scoop is the best helping tool in shaping sushi balls. If you don't have one in hand, you can simply take 40g of sushi rice, wrap in plastic wrap, and round with your hands.
  • My sushi roll is ready! but the rice sticks to my knife and I can't cut smoothly...
  • A wet blade is the key to beautiful, non-stressed cutting, as sushi rice is stickier than plain rice to maintain its rolled shape. Wet a paper towel and wipe your blade everytime you make a cut.
  • I want to make my sushi look like ones in restaurants!
  • Practice makes perfect, but serving is important as well. You can use a black dish to enhance the colour of your sushi.
  • Here is the recommendation from Japan's Imperial tableware brand.
  • I made too much... How can I save leftover sushi?
  • Wrap in plastic wrap and keep in cool temperature. The refrigerator is not recommended, as it will dry out rice. Fridged rice can fall apart and lose its delicate taste.

CHOOSE PLATES

One epitome of sushi is that is goes well with any kinds of plates. Round or squre, large or small, plain or patterned, there exists myriads of options.

Let's begin with plates, and you can coordinate other items around them.

! HINTS

●●● ROUND is most common and universal shape. Its minimalist aesthetic delivers a canvas that naturally draws your eyes to the center, where your sushi is placed. You can serve multiple sushi and enjoy arrangements, or you can put just a few and decorate the expanse.

■■■ SQUARE is rare but effective in combination with other plates. Its straight edge creates a contrast on the table when paired with a soft, round plate. Perfect for presenting sushi as an appetizer.
